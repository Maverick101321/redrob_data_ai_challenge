Problem Framing

The Challenge — and the Traps

What the problem asks
  • Given a single Job Description for a Senior AI/ML Engineer (5–9 yrs, production retrieval systems)
  • Rank the best 100 candidates from a pool of 100,000 synthetic profiles
  • Include per-candidate reasoning (1–2 sentences, specific, honest)
  • Produce a CSV output — no hallucinations, no identical reasoning strings
Trap 1 — Honeypot Profiles (~80)

Candidates with subtly impossible timelines — e.g. 8 yrs exp at a company founded 3 yrs ago, or "expert" in 10 skills with 0 months usage. Keyword matchers rank them highly. We detect and eliminate them.

Trap 2 — Keyword Stuffers

A "Marketing Manager" who lists FAISS, Pinecone, and LoRA as skills. Perfect keyword match, zero role fit. Our coherence check penalises unrelated titles with packed AI skill lists.

Trap 3 — Dead Candidates

Great on paper but inactive for 8 months, 5% recruiter response rate. Behaviorally unavailable. We apply a multiplicative penalty rather than ignoring this signal entirely.

Why semantic is 35%

A candidate who built FAISS-based retrieval systems may not use the word "FAISS" in their skills list — they describe what they did in career narrative. Semantic similarity catches this; keyword matching doesn't.

Skill score internals

Each skill is weighted by: JD relevance (must-have = 1.0, nice-to-have = 0.4) × proficiency (expert→1.0, beginner→0.25) × duration factor (capped at 18 months). Assessment scores from Redrob blend in at 30%.

Experience score internals

Bell curve peaks at 6–8 yrs. Penalises pure consulting careers (TCS/Infosys/Wipro/Accenture etc.) at 0.70×. Boosts candidates whose career descriptions contain production deployment keywords: "deployed", "at scale", "real users", "latency".

Data Quality

Honeypot Detection — Hard Filters

Check 1 — Timeline Inflation

Total duration_months across all roles vs stated years_of_experience × 12. If ratio > 1.35 → flagged. Accounts for role overlap slack of 18 months.

Check 2 — Expert with Zero Duration

Claims "expert" proficiency on 4+ skills with duration_months = 0. A real expert has used the skill — zero months means they listed it without ever using it.

Check 3 — Education Timeline Conflict

Graduation year is later than implied career start year (with 2-year buffer for co-ops). e.g., graduated 2022 but claims 10 years experience.

Check 4 — Single Role Impossibility

One role's duration alone exceeds total stated experience. e.g., one role is 120 months but total experience is claimed as 5 years.

Why multiplicative, not additive?

Adding behavioral signals to the score lets a great-on-paper but dead candidate still rank high. Multiplying means a candidate inactive for a year gets their entire composite score cut in half — behaviorally unavailable talent is functionally unusable, regardless of their skills.

Semantic Understanding

Why Semantic Search Beats Keywords

The model — all-MiniLM-L6-v2
  • 22MB — tiny, fast, CPU-friendly
  • 384-dimensional dense embeddings
  • Pre-trained on 1B+ sentence pairs
  • Cosine similarity via dot product (both vectors normalised)
  • 100K candidates scored in milliseconds once pre-computed
Candidate text construction

We concatenate: headline | summary | career descriptions (all roles) | skills with proficiency + duration | certifications. Career descriptions carry the heaviest semantic weight because they describe actual work done, not just claimed keywords.

Example: keyword match fails, semantic wins

Verified embedding statistics
  • Embedding matrix: (100,000 × 384) float32 → 146 MB
  • All vectors L2-normalised — dot product = cosine similarity
  • Top semantic score in full dataset: 0.9105
  • Semantic scoring step runtime: < 1 second (vectorised numpy)

Output Quality & Compliance

Reasoning Generation & Compute Budget

Reasoning — 4 strict rules
  • Only mentions skills present in the candidate profile — no hallucination
  • Every string is unique — pulls actual company names, skill names, durations, scores
  • No pure name-insertion templates ("Alice is a good fit…")
  • Rank 1 reasoning reads like rank 1 — tone is calibrated to composite score
Example output

"6.0 yrs experience; most recently as NLP Engineer at Uber (2y 3mo); with JD-relevant skills: FAISS (advanced, 35mo), Pinecone (expert, 88mo). Actively open to work; active 3d ago; 84% recruiter response rate; GitHub activity score 72/100."

Compute constraints — all met
  • No GPU during ranking step
  • No hosted LLM API calls (no OpenAI, Claude, etc.)
  • No network during ranking
  • 16 GB RAM sufficient (embeddings = 146 MB, scoring = pure Python)
  • Ranking step < 5 minutes (semantic: <1s, scoring: ~2 min streaming)
